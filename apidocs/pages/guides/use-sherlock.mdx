The assistant (codename **Sherlock**) is a built-in analyst you can talk to in plain English. Ask a question about your evaluation data and it writes the query for you, runs it, and replies with a short written answer plus a chart or table when the numbers call for one.

You never write SQL. You never pick a chart type. You ask a question the way you'd ask a teammate, and the assistant does the rest — grounded only in your data, for the app you're currently in.

## What it can do

The assistant is **scoped to one app at a time** (for example your voice, bot, or sales app) and is **read-only** — it looks at your evaluation results but never changes them. Within that scope it can:

- **Answer analytics questions** — counts, trends, breakdowns, comparisons over your evaluation data.
- **Show the answer as a chart or table** — it decides the right shape (bar, line, pie, KPI number, or a plain table) for you.
- **Hold a conversation** — it remembers what you just asked, so follow-ups like "now break that down by week" work without repeating yourself.
- **Propose workflow edits** — when you're inside the workflow builder in edit mode and have permission to manage workflows, you can ask it to draft changes to the canvas. It only *proposes*; you review and save.

It will politely decline questions that aren't about your data, and it will ask one clarifying question if your request is ambiguous rather than guessing.

## Asking a good question

Be specific about **what**, **how to group it**, and **over what period**. The more concrete the ask, the tighter the answer.

Example questions you can paste in as-is:

```text
How many evaluations did we run last week?
Show me the average score by evaluator over the last 30 days.
Which days had the most failed runs this month?
Break down evaluation outcomes by status as a pie chart.
What's the trend in pass rate week over week?
Compare this week's volume to last week's.
```

Tips:

- **Name a time window** ("last 7 days", "in May") so the assistant doesn't have to guess.
- **Say how to slice it** ("by evaluator", "by status", "by day").
- If you only care about the top results, say so ("top 10 by volume"). The assistant is honest about cardinality — if there's more data than it showed, it'll tell you "top N of more".

## Reading the answer

Every reply has up to three parts:

| Part | What it is |
|---|---|
| **Written answer** | A direct 1–3 sentence summary. Key numbers are bolded. |
| **Chart or table** | A rendered card the assistant chose for the data — a bar/line/pie chart, a single KPI number, or a plain table. |
| **Sources** | References to the underlying rows, shown separately so the prose stays clean. |

The numbers in the written answer always come from the chart or table — the assistant never invents figures. If a question genuinely has no data, it says so plainly instead of showing an empty chart.

## Following up

The assistant remembers the current conversation, so you can keep refining without re-stating context. Each of these works as a follow-up to an earlier question:

```text
Now group that by week instead of by day.
Just the top 5.
Show that as a line chart.
What about for the previous month?
And for the failed ones only?
```

References like "those reps", "that period", or "the same chart but..." are resolved automatically from the conversation so far.

If a thread gets long, you may occasionally see a short "summary" separator in the chat — that's the assistant compacting older context to stay fast. Your conversation continues seamlessly; nothing is lost.

## Proposing workflow changes (edit mode)

When you're in the **workflow builder**, viewing a workflow in **edit mode**, and you have permission to manage workflows, the assistant gains an extra ability: it can **propose edits to the canvas**.

Ask in plain English, for example:

```text
Add a step that waits 3 days before the next message.
Insert a WhatsApp message after the call step.
Change the wait on this node to 15 minutes.
```

The assistant returns the change as a **proposed canvas patch** for you to review. It will **never** save or publish on its own — you apply the change manually after looking it over. If a workflow is very large, the assistant may ask you to open the specific node you want to edit and try again.

This ability only appears when all three conditions are met (builder open, edit mode, manage permission). Outside the builder, the assistant stays focused on answering questions about your data.

## Where to find it

The assistant lives as a chat panel inside your app's workspace. Admin pages don't show it, since they have no app data context.

## For developers

The chat is powered by a streaming API. Your messages post to a streaming endpoint, and the conversation is rebuilt from a stored event log so a refresh never loses the thread.

See the [API Reference](/docs/api):

- `POST /api/report-builder/v2/chat/stream` — send a message and stream the assistant's reply.
- `GET /api/report-builder/v2/sessions/{session_id}` — load a chat session.
- `GET /api/report-builder/v2/sessions/{session_id}/parts` — replay a session's event stream (used to rehydrate the conversation).
- `POST /api/report-builder/v2/sessions/{session_id}/turns/{turn_id}/cancel` — cancel an in-progress reply.
