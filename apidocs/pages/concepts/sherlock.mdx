Sherlock is the platform's built-in assistant. You talk to it in plain English — "how many calls did we run last week?", "show win rate by rep" — and it figures out how to answer, queries your data safely, and replies with a short written answer plus a chart or table when one helps.

It is **conversational** (it remembers what you asked earlier in the chat), **read-only** (it never changes your evaluation data), and **scoped to you** — it only ever sees data for your tenant, your app, and your account. You don't need to know SQL, table names, or anything about how the data is stored.

This page explains what Sherlock can do and how it works, in plain terms.

## What you can ask

Sherlock is focused on the data and workflows inside the app you're currently using (for example a calling app or a chat-bot app). It is good at:

- **Analytics questions** — counts, rates, breakdowns, trends, and comparisons over your evaluation results. "What's the average score this month?", "Break down outcomes by agent", "Compare this week to last week."
- **Follow-ups in context** — you can say "now just the failed ones" or "those reps from before" and it understands what you mean, because it keeps track of the conversation.
- **Workflow edits (in the builder)** — when you're editing a workflow on the canvas, Sherlock can *propose* changes to it. More on this below.

If a question is unclear, Sherlock asks you one short clarifying question instead of guessing. If a question isn't about your data (just chit-chat, or something the data can't answer), it tells you so politely rather than inventing a number.

## How an answer gets built

Behind the scenes, Sherlock works as a small team: a **supervisor** that runs the conversation, and focused **specialists** it hands work to. You don't manage any of this — it's automatic — but it helps to know the shape of it.

    U[You ask a question] --> S[Supervisor]
    S --> P[Plan: understand & break down the question]
    P -->|analytics| D[Data specialist: write a safe query, build a chart]
    P -->|workflow edit| A[Authoring specialist: propose canvas changes]
    D --> R[Answer: text + chart/table]
    A --> R
    R --> U
`} />

1. **Understand** — Sherlock first rewrites your question so it stands on its own (resolving "those", "last week", and so on), and decides whether it's answerable, needs clarification, or is off-topic.
2. **Plan** — it breaks the request into one or more sub-questions and routes each to the right specialist.
3. **Query safely** — for analytics, the **data specialist** writes a database query, which is checked by a safety validator (see below) *before* it runs.
4. **Visualize** — results are turned into a chart, KPI, table, or summary automatically. The right chart type is chosen for you.
5. **Answer** — you get a concise written answer with the chart or table attached, and the numbers always come from the real result — never made up.

You'll see this happen live as you watch: Sherlock streams its steps (planning, querying, charting) into the chat as it goes.

## Read-only and safely scoped

Two guarantees matter most:

- **Read-only.** Sherlock can look at your data but never edits or deletes it. Every query it runs is checked to be a read, restricted to the tables it's allowed to see, and filtered to your tenant and app. A query that doesn't meet these rules is rejected before it ever touches the database.
- **Per-tenant, per-user.** Sherlock only sees what you can see. It can't reach another tenant's or another user's data.

Sherlock can also only query a **curated set of fields** that have been described for your app — not the entire raw database. This keeps answers reliable and meaningful, and means it won't surface half-empty or confusing columns.

## Charts you can trust

When a result is best shown visually, Sherlock builds the chart for you and picks the type — bar, grouped/stacked bar, line, area, or pie — based on the shape of the data. Some results are better as a single KPI number, a table, or a short summary, and Sherlock chooses that too. You never have to configure a chart; you just get the one that fits.

## Editing workflows with Sherlock

When you're **editing a workflow** in the builder canvas, Sherlock gains an extra ability: it can suggest changes to that workflow from a plain-English request, like "add a follow-up message two days later."

Two things are always true here:

- It only appears when you're actually in edit mode and you have permission to manage workflows.
- It **proposes** edits — it never saves or publishes on its own. You review the suggested change and apply it yourself.

## Remembering the conversation

Sherlock keeps the thread of your conversation across turns, so you can keep refining without repeating yourself. Long conversations are automatically condensed in the background so context isn't lost — you'll occasionally see a small "summarized earlier messages" marker in the chat when this happens. There's nothing to manage; it just keeps working.

## Using it through the API

In the product you simply type in the chat panel. If you're integrating programmatically, the assistant is served by the report-builder v2 endpoints (the route group the assistant runs under). Messages stream back as you'd see them in the UI.

```bash
POST /api/report-builder/v2/chat/stream
```

To load or re-open a past conversation and its messages:

```bash
GET /api/report-builder/v2/sessions/{session_id}
GET /api/report-builder/v2/sessions/{session_id}/parts
```

To stop a turn that's still running:

```bash
POST /api/report-builder/v2/sessions/{session_id}/turns/{turn_id}/cancel
```

See the [API Reference](/docs/api) for full request and response details — `POST /api/report-builder/v2/chat/stream`, `GET /api/report-builder/v2/sessions/{session_id}`, and `GET /api/report-builder/v2/sessions/{session_id}/parts`.

## In short

| You do | Sherlock does |
|---|---|
| Ask in plain English | Understands and plans the question |
| Refine with follow-ups | Remembers the conversation |
| Read the answer | Queries safely, read-only, scoped to you |
| Glance at the chart | Picks and builds the right visual |
| Edit a workflow | Proposes canvas changes for you to apply |
